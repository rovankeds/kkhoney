About KK Honey

The outbreak of COVID 19 in Uganda escalated the need for the counter natural measures of protecting the body against attack by the virus which required boosting body immunity to be able to survive alongside other means. Many people discovered the power of natural products such as lemons, ginger and honey and concluded that the combination of the three can effectively boost body immunity against the virus and can easily encounter it and this was manifested by a lot of people stoking these products during the period up to the present. The period of COVID which we are still facing is characterized with common symptoms like flue and coughs which three products can fight and these are common among many people up to the present. The business there fore embarked on these facts to establish a farm to produce honey in large quantities and use it to produce honey cough syrup with a mix of ginger targeting boosting immunity of people against COVID 19 and also produces a honey jelly and lip bum as cosmetic product to counter skin issues.



Vision

Our Vision is to become one of the leading commercial bee farms and leading supplier of honey products highly recognized in both Uganda and international markets



Mission

Our mission is to create Quality honey products and offer free apiculture skills to community members to alleviate poverty and Value addition to Uganda produced honey products to increase quality and increase access to the market.



Goals of the business

Improvement of honey Quality and Sustainability of honey Production
Equipping local people/ residents with modern bee farming technique
To realize livelihoods for many more among families
To introduce a further 100 local people to beekeeping as a means of providing personal income and reducing local poverty.
Improvement of local health standards through the use of bee products such as honey and cough syrups
To create income generating opportunities through honey production for the youth to be able to earn income
To provide opportunity for learning and understanding of honey-bee integration
